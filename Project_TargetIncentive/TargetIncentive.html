Using Customer Data to Optimize Incentive Deployment and Reduce Cancellations at Buckeye Resorts

Description:

This project aimed to reduce the financial losses incurred by Buckeye Resorts, Inc. (BRI) due to reservation cancellations. By leveraging customer data, the objective was to predict the likelihood of a reservation being canceled and improve the effectiveness of incentive programs designed to prevent such cancellations.

Technical Methods and Skills Used:

  • Data Collection and Preprocessing: Analyzed a dataset containing 119,391 customer reservations over three years (2015-2017), including 32 attributes for each reservation. Conducted exploratory data analysis to understand the data and identify relevant features for modeling.
  • Feature Selection: Chose key customer attributes for the predictive model:
    • Predictor Variable: is_canceled: Indicator of whether a customer reservation was canceled.
    • Explanatory Variables:
      • kidsToAdults: Ratio of the number of kids to adults.
      • cancelRate: (previous_bookings_not_canceled - previous_bookings_canceled) / total number of bookings.
      • correctRoom: Logical indicator where reserved_room_type equals assigned_room_type.
  • Model Development: Developed a cost matrix to evaluate the benefits of different predictive models. Implemented a k-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) model to predict reservation cancellations, focusing on achieving high accuracy at low cost.

Outcome:

  • Model Results: The KNN model demonstrated high accuracy in predicting cancellations using the selected variables, offering a cost-effective solution for BRI. The model's confusion matrix indicated a strong predictive performance, justifying the approach.
  • Recommendations and Discussion: Recommended that BRI proceed with the proposed data-driven approach to predict and mitigate reservation cancellations. Suggested conducting trials with varying incentive amounts ($50, $100, $150) over a year to determine the most profitable incentive amount, as current data was insufficient to conclusively establish the optimal compensation.
  • Conclusion: Successfully constructed a predictive model using is_canceled as the predictor variable and kidsToAdults, cancelRate, and correctRoom as explanatory variables. Confirmed that implementing some form of compensation can benefit reservation follow-through. Proposed further trials with different incentive amounts to refine the approach and maximize profit from reservations.

Interactive Features: Implemented interactive visualizations and dashboards to allow BRI to monitor model predictions and adjust incentive strategies in real-time.

Feedback and Iteration: Gathered feedback from stakeholders and iteratively improved the model and incentive strategy based on real-world performance and user input.
